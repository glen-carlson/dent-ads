Thesis
Two operating models compared · Fritz's observed rules vs MQL-optimised best practice
Fritz's Operating Model (Observed)
Optimise for Cost Per Lead
Primary KPI is CPL. Ads paused when CPL exceeds ~£13 (EMEA), ~A$39 (APAC).
Question: LBP campaigns are showing £8 CPL — but when we look at which leads actually qualify, only 15-19% become MQLs. That's ~£76 per qualified lead. Meanwhile you paused ads at £16-25 CPL that had 74-89% MQL rates. How do you weigh lead cost against lead quality when deciding what to pause?
Calendar-Based Rotation
Rotate creatives every 2-4 weeks regardless of performance. Kills winners for freshness.
Question: NarrowPath Opportunity Yes had a 74% MQL rate and £16.73 CPMQL when you paused it. What was the signal that told you it was time to rotate? Was there a frequency spike, a CTR drop, or something else — or is it a time-based cycle? And if it's time-based, how do you decide the right cycle length?
Flood & Cull
Launch many variants simultaneously. Kill zero-lead ads at £10-25 spend. Keep what survives.
Question: When you launch a batch of new ads, what's the kill threshold? If an ad has spent £15 with zero leads you'd pause it — but what about an ad with 5 leads and zero MQLs at £50 spend? At what point does lead quality factor into the cull decision, not just lead volume?
Frequency Not Tracked
No evidence of frequency or CTR decay as pause triggers. Decisions appear CPL-only.
Question: Do you track frequency as a leading indicator? When you see an ad's frequency climbing above 2 or 3, does that change your decision on whether to pause or scale it? Or is CPL the main thing you're watching day to day?
Meetings Not Tracked
No evidence that meeting bookings influence ad decisions. Volume of leads is the proxy for success.
Question: Selling Time For Money was generating 10 meeting bookings per week when it was paused. Do you look at meeting data when deciding what to keep running, or is it mainly leads and CPL? If an ad has a high CPL but is generating meetings at £80 each, would that change the decision?
MQL-Optimised Model (Best Practice)
Optimise for Cost Per Qualified Lead
Primary KPI is CPMQL. Pause when CPMQL > £40 for 7 days. CPL is a secondary indicator only.
Performance-Based Rotation
Rotate when MQL rate drops below 30% for 5 consecutive days. Winners run until they fatigue — not on a calendar.
Test & Scale
Scale when CPMQL < £15 AND MQL rate > 40%. Kill at £25 spend with zero MQLs — not zero leads.
Fatigue = Frequency + MQL Decline
Rotate when frequency > 3.0 AND MQL rate drops 25%+ from its 7-day peak. Both must be true. One number to watch, one trend to confirm.
Meetings as Ultimate KPI
Cost per meeting booked < £100. An ad that generates meetings at £80 each is worth more than one generating leads at £5 each.
